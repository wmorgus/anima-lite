### PIN-46 — the coordinate backbone: one shared spine under material/formal/efficient ("spine under the spine")

Spun out of PIN-45's movement-map discussion. Once `movement.md` (formal cause's deep tier, PIN-45) was placed as a spine file, the natural next question was whether material and efficient deserve the same top-level/deep-tier split — a data-lineage deep-tier under `material.md` (which entity/field is created/read/mutated at which point, and by what) and an invocation-graph deep-tier under `efficient.md` (what mechanism/trigger/handler literally fires at each step — distinct from formal's *logical* choreography of what-follows-what).

The real fork this pin owns: **three separate deep files, each with its own coordinate scheme** (safer, smaller, but risks the three grids drifting out of step with each other — exactly what uno-blueprint's paired-write anti-drift rule exists to prevent) — versus **one shared coordinate spine** (`scenario × path × step`, the same grid `movement.md` already establishes) with three column-families hanging off each cell: formal's cell = what activity/boundary acts, material's cell = what data that step touches, efficient's cell = what mechanism executes it. Operator's read (2026-07-14): the second option, explicitly — "it's like the actual spine under the spine." Single coordinate scaffold, no independent grids to fall out of sync, telos remains the top-level sentence governing the whole backbone.

Not yet designed: the concrete schema for the shared grid (what a "cell" record actually holds across three column-families — is it one row with three fields, or a genuine join across three tables/sections); whether every repo's spine gets this backbone or only ones probed as part of an arete founding pass (same generalization question PIN-45 already resolved for `movement.md` alone — likely inherits the same shape: available to `ari-map` generally, required rigor for arete); how this interacts with `ari-map`'s existing per-cause file structure without breaking the existing `telos.md`-as-entry-point / cause-files-as-reference-depth pattern; whether this is a `HARNESS.md` §2 spec-ownership addition (a new canonical shape doc, likely owned by `ari-map/SKILL.md`'s Output section, same as the existing spine formats).

# The pipeline is a four-cause decomposition of "make a software change"

Captured: 2026-07-07 (Will). Status: **ratified observation — propagated to PHILOSOPHY.md 2026-07-07.**

---

## The claim

The skill decomposition is the four-cause description of "make a software change," where software is an argument:

| Skill | Cause | Of the change |
|---|---|---|
| **ari-intake** | **final** | what the change is for — telos sharpened, everything argued for |
| **ari-map** | **material** | what the change is made from and into — the mapped codebase as matter |
| **ari-argue** | **formal** | what the change IS — the contract as its essence: which claims, what promise |
| **ari-port** | **efficient** | what brings form into matter — execution FROM the contract (the builder works from the form; skill name kept per vocab decision 2b, the work-type noun generalizes) |

The harness applied the four causes to repos (the spine files); the pipeline itself instantiates the same four causes over the change. The method is its own metaphysics — the tool's architecture recapitulates its diagnostic lens.

## Why this is load-bearing, not decoration

**1. It predicted intake.** The pipeline ran for four calibration runs with its final cause implicit — borrowed from the prototype's code, which carried the argument pre-made. Ripple removed the prototype and the seam appeared immediately (see `intake.md`). Intake wasn't invented to serve ripple; it was discovered — the decomposition was incomplete until the final cause got its own skill. A frame that predicts the missing piece before you name it is earning its keep.

**2. It makes the pipeline's completeness arguable.** The skills are complete for the same reason the spine is complete: four causes tile explanation. A proposed fifth skill must either name a fifth cause (falsifying the frame) or decompose into these four (confirming it). ari-backlog is no counterexample — it is orthogonal by design, not part of "make a software change"; it brackets the pipeline. Validation/coherence-check is no counterexample either — verifying that form landed in matter is the completion of the efficient cause's own work (the builder checks the house against the blueprint); it lives inside port.

**3. The levels nest without conflict.** ari-map produces a four-cause spine yet occupies the material slot — no contradiction. Relative to the *change*, the mapped codebase is matter; the spine's internal four-cause structure is about the *repo*. Matter at one level has its own form and telos at another (bricks are formed matter; still the matter of the house). Standard move, and it is why the same lens works at both altitudes.

**4. Two teloi, one gate.** The repo has a telos (spine `telos.md`); the change has a telos (intake's output). GATE-TELOS is literally where the two meet — it fires on conflict between the change's purpose and the repo's. Under this frame the gate's placement inside intake (early, before any contracting) is not a convenience; it is where that meeting structurally belongs.

**5. Pipeline order is the classical order.** intake→map→argue→port = final→material→formal→efficient. The final cause is first in intention, last in realization — the telos leads and everything downstream is oriented by it, which is the same commitment PHILOSOPHY.md already holds for the spine ("if you don't know the telos, you can't sort substrate from claim").
